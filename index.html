Special Rules

ห นำ — Leading ห

When ห precedes a Low Class sonorant (ม น ง ว ล ร ย ญ ณ), it is silent but shifts the tone class from Low → High, giving High Class tone patterns (patterns 6–9).

  • หมา — dog (ม is LC, ห นำ → Rising)
  • หนู — mouse (น is LC, ห นำ → Rising)
  • หนาว — cold (น is LC, ห นำ → Rising)

Live vs Dead Syllables

  • Live: ends in long vowel or sonorant final (ม น ง ว ย) → tone follows no-mark pattern
  • Dead — short: short vowel + stop final (ก ด ต บ ป) → High (LC) or Low (HC/MC)
  • Dead — long: long vowel + stop final → Falling (LC) or Low (HC/MC)
  • Final sonorants do NOT affect tone class — only the initial consonant's class matters

Consonant Clusters

When two consonants begin a syllable (e.g. กล กร ปร), the first consonant (C1) determines the tone class. C2 is pronounced but does not affect tone.

Looped vs Loopless Script

Thai has two main written styles. Looped (มีหัว) — the traditional style with small loops/serifs at stroke endings, used in most textbooks and formal writing. Loopless (ไม่มีหัว) — the modern sans-serif style seen on signage, apps, and casual media. The characters are the same; only the style differs.
